<p><ul><li>Colorimetric Method</li><li>Electrode Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Portable Ammonia Nitrogen Analyzers market comprises two primary types: the colorimetric method and the electrode method. The colorimetric method involves a chemical reaction that produces a color change proportional to ammonia concentration, allowing for visual or photometric measurement. In contrast, the electrode method utilizes ion-selective electrodes that directly measure ammonia levels in a sample through electrical conductivity. Both methods are crucial for field applications in water quality monitoring and wastewater management, offering efficiency and accuracy in real-time measurements.</p></p>
